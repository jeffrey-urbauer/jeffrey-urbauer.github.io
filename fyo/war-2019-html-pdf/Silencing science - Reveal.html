Silencing science

Co-produced with

Apple Podcasts Google Podcasts Stitcher Spotify RadioPublic RSS

President Donald Trump says he doubts humans have much of a role in climate change. His administration has downplayed the science of climate change and sought to silence scientists working for the federal government. In this hour, Reveal’s Elizabeth Shogren details the pressures one researcher faced as she worked on a project for the National Park Service.

A big storm could submerge Canaveral National Seashore

If greenhouse gas emissions keep growing and a category 3 hurricane were to hit Florida’s Canaveral National Seashore in 2050, the whole national park will be under water, according to projections by scientist Maria Caffrey and the National Park Service. Thousands of sea turtle nests could be be washed away, and archaeological sites, park buildings, roads and boardwalks all will be at risk.

Calculations of sea level rise and storm surge can help park service personnel protect natural and historic treasures at parks and help the public know what’s at stake in climate change. But under President Trump, the park service long suppressed this research. Now that it’s out, the agency hasn’t publicly spread the word that this trail-blazing science is available for 118 national parks.
